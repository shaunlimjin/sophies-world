🤯 Weird But True

3 Facts That Will Blow Your Mind

🦜 The World's Chubbiest Parrot Can't Fly! The kākāpō parrot from New Zealand can weigh up to 9 pounds — the same as a newborn baby — and it cannot fly at all. It just climbs trees and slides down. It also only has babies when one special tree grows lots of berries, which happens only once every few years!
🐌 A Tiny Snail Has More Teeth Than a Shark! The limpet (a little sea snail) has over 750 teeth — way more than a shark's 300! And those tiny teeth are made of the strongest natural material ever discovered. Stronger than spider silk!
🪼 This Jellyfish Never Has to Die! There's a jellyfish called Turritopsis dohrnii that can technically live forever. When it gets old or sick, it shrinks back into a tiny blob and starts its whole life over again — like pressing the undo button on aging!

🌍 World Watch

Two Big Things Happening in the World Right Now

🛢️ Story 1: A War That's Making Everything More Expensive

Since February 2026, the United States and Iran have been in a serious conflict. The US and Israel launched airstrikes on Iran, and Iran fought back by blocking a very important stretch of water called the Strait of Hormuz — a narrow gap in the ocean just 21 miles wide. Here's why it matters: 20% of all the world's oil travels through that gap every single day. Right now, instead of 130+ ships crossing it daily, only about 9 are getting through.

When oil can't move freely, prices for gas — and for everything that gets delivered by truck — start to go up. The IMF (a big global money organisation) warned this week that if things don't improve, the whole world could head into a recession, which means businesses slow down and life gets harder for a lot of families everywhere.

💡 Think of it this way: imagine the only hallway to your school cafeteria got blocked, so nobody could get lunch. Now imagine that hallway was the only path for the world's fuel — that's the Strait of Hormuz.

🛍️ Story 2: Why Your Toys and Clothes Might Cost More Soon

The US government has put a 145% tariff on things made in China — which is a huge extra tax. So if a toy costs $10 to make, the government adds almost $14.50 more before it hits the store shelf. Walmart, Amazon, and Target have already started raising prices. Researchers say American families are paying about $1,500 extra per year because of these taxes.

A lot of things we use every day — phones, shoes, backpacks — are made in China. The US and China are in a big disagreement about trade (how countries buy and sell from each other), and right now both sides are refusing to budge. The leaders of both countries may meet in May to talk it out.

💡 A tariff is like a cover charge at a party — except it's for products from other countries. The more you charge, the fewer people (or products) come in.

💰 Money Moves

The Secret Trick Smart Savers Know: Pay Yourself First!

Most people spend their money first and save whatever's left over. But guess what? Usually nothing is left! 😅

Smart savers do it backwards: the moment they get money — allowance, birthday money, anything — they put a piece of it in savings before spending anything. This is called "Pay Yourself First."

💡 Try this: If you save just $2 a week, by the end of the year you'd have $104 — and if it's in a bank account earning interest, it grows even more without you doing anything extra! 🏦

Cool kid entrepreneur: A girl named Gabby Goodwin got tired of her hair barrettes falling out all the time. So she and her mom invented GaBBY Bows — a double-snap barrette that actually stayed in! She started with a small idea, used her savings to invest in it, and turned it into a real business. Every big business starts with one problem someone decided to solve. Can You Figure This Out?

In Story 1 this week, you learned that the Strait of Hormuz is only 21 miles wide at its narrowest point, but 20% of the world's oil passes through it every day.

If the whole world uses 100 million barrels of oil every day, how many barrels travel through the Strait of Hormuz? And if Iran is only allowing 9 ships through per day (instead of 130), what fraction of normal traffic is still getting through? 🚢🛢️
