🤯 Weird But True

Nature Is SO Strange! 😲

🐟 The Parrotfish Sleeps in a Slime Bubble! Every single night, the parrotfish wraps itself inside a big bubble of its own mucus (yes, like snot!) to sleep. Within 30 minutes, it's tucked inside a slimy sleeping bag that keeps hungry predators away. Scientists think the smell confuses sharks and moray eels. Sweet slime dreams! 😴
♾️ This Jellyfish Can Live Forever! The immortal jellyfish is the only known animal that can reverse its own aging. When it gets old or sick, it transforms back into a tiny baby jellyfish and starts life all over again — like hitting the rewind button! Scientists are studying it to understand aging in all animals, including humans.
🟫 Wombats Poop in Cubes! Wombats are the only animals in the world that make cube-shaped poop. Scientists figured out that their intestines have stretchy sections that squeeze droppings into little squares. Wombats stack these cube-poops on rocks to mark their territory. Nature's weirdest building blocks! 🏗️

🚢 The World's Most Important Doorway Opens Again

The Strait of Hormuz is a narrow strip of ocean between Iran and Oman — only about 33 miles wide at its thinnest point. But it's one of the most important places on Earth: about 20% of all the world's oil passes through it every single day. In early 2026, Iran closed this waterway to ships during a conflict with the United States and Israel. Oil prices spiked and countries around the world started worrying about energy and food costs.

This week, on April 17, Iran announced the strait was open again as part of a two-week ceasefire agreement. Oil prices dropped by 11% in just one day — one of the biggest drops in years! World leaders from 40 countries met virtually to talk about keeping the peace.

🤔 Think of it like this: Imagine if someone put a giant padlock on the entrance to the biggest supermarket in town. Suddenly, fewer trucks can bring in food, prices go up, and everyone panics. When the lock comes off — whew! — everything starts flowing again. The Strait of Hormuz is that supermarket door, but for the whole world's energy supply.

🕊️ The Fight in Ukraine Continues — Russia Launches Massive Attack

On April 16, Russia launched hundreds of drones and dozens of missiles at cities across Ukraine. The attacks lasted for hours through the night and hit neighborhoods and homes, killing at least 16 people. Ukraine's air defenses worked hard to shoot many of them down, but it's been one of the biggest attacks of the war so far this year.

The conflict between Russia and Ukraine has been going on since February 2022. Many world leaders, including the United States and countries in Europe, are working to find ways to bring peace to Ukraine. It's a reminder that while some parts of the world are finding ceasefires, other conflicts are still ongoing.

🤔 Imagine two neighbors who got into a really serious argument that turned into a long, painful fight. Other neighbors keep trying to help stop it, but it's very hard when both sides are angry. Countries around the world are like those neighbors, trying to find a way to make the fighting stop for good.

Amazing Things About Your Heritage! 🌺

🌊 The World's Tallest Indoor Waterfall Is in Singapore!

Inside Changi Airport, there's a jaw-dropping waterfall called the HSBC Rain Vortex — it's 40 meters tall, which is like a 13-story building of falling water, completely INDOORS! It's inside a place called Jewel Changi, which also has a trampoline park, a giant slide, an indoor forest, a movie theater, and a swimming pool. Changi Airport has been voted the world's best airport many years in a row. It's not just an airport — it's an adventure! ✈️

🦁 The "Lion City" That Never Had Lions!

Singapore's name "Singapura" means "Lion City" in Sanskrit — but here's the twist: wild lions have never lived in Singapore! Hundreds of years ago, a prince named Sang Nila Utama spotted a mysterious creature and thought it was a lion. He was so impressed he named the island after it. But historians think what he actually saw was a Malayan tiger! The name stuck forever, and today Singapore proudly wears its lion symbol everywhere — even on its national flag's crest, the Merlion (half lion, half fish). 🐯

🚀 Astronauts Splash Down Near San Diego! Just last week, four brave astronauts from NASA's Artemis II mission returned from flying around the moon and splashed down in the Pacific Ocean, right off the coast of San Diego, California! Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Canadian astronaut Jeremy Hansen had just completed a 10-day journey — traveling 252,756 miles from Earth, the farthest any humans have been from our planet in over 50 years! After their capsule landed in the ocean, they were picked up by a Navy ship and cheered as heroes. 🎉

🛸 California's New Space Museum Is OPEN! The California Science Center in Los Angeles just unveiled its incredible new Samuel Oschin Air and Space Center. The star attraction? A full-sized Space Shuttle standing UPRIGHT in a 200-foot-tall tower — you can see it from miles away! If you're ever in LA, this is a must-visit for any space lover. 💰 Money Moves

Let's Talk About PROFIT! 📈

Here's a word every business person needs to know: PROFIT. Profit is the money you have left over after paying for everything it cost you to make or get what you sold.

Say you make lemonade 🍋. You spend $3 on lemons and sugar. Then you sell 10 cups at $1 each, earning $10. Your profit = $10 − $3 = $7. That $7 is yours! The more you sell without spending more, the bigger your profit. That's how businesses grow — and it's how kids your age are already making real money!

💡 Sophie's Money Tip: Before starting any business, make a simple plan: What will I sell? What will it cost me? How much will I charge? If your price is higher than your cost, you're on track to earn a profit. Try it with lemonade, bracelets, art, or whatever you love making!
